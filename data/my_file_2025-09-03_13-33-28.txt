- **LLM**: An AI system trained on vast amounts of text data to perform tasks like answering questions, summarizing content, and translating languages.

### Architecture and Functionality
- **Deep Learning Architectures**: Typically transformer-based neural networks.
- **Learning**: Patterns, grammar, context, and reasoning abilities from training data.

- **Knowledge Limitation**: Limited to initial training data.

- **Standardized Interfaces**: Frameworks like Model Context Protocol (MCP) allow seamless connections with various tools.

### Integration Challenges and Solutions
- **Custom Solutions**: Previously required for each tool combination.
- **MCP**: Provides a universal connector, reducing the need for multiple custom integrations.

### Practical Example
- **Real-Time Weather Data**: An LLM can use MCP to interact with any compatible weather service, simplifying development.

- **Powerful AI Systems**: Capable of understanding and generating human language.
